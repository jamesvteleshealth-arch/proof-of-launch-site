Why the switch is correct
You're re-ordering by the sequence buyers will actually experience.
The current order (Studio first, LaunchOps second) implies a narrative: "We build apps, and we also QA them." That framing makes Studio the hero product and LaunchOps the supporting act. It made sense when you were thinking about this as a dev studio that happens to have QA capability.
The switch to LaunchOps first, Studio second implies a different narrative: "We prove AI-built apps are ready to ship — and we also build them when that's what you need." LaunchOps becomes the hero, Studio becomes the expansion. This matches three things better than the current order:
1. It matches where your market window actually is. The vibe-coder QA specialist lane is the one with the Apple App Store tailwind, the documented pain, the competitive opening, and the fastest sales cycle. The Studio is where Studio operators live; LaunchOps is where the market is urgently buying right now. Leading with LaunchOps is leading with the thing prospects will actually search for.
2. It matches your primary sales strategy. You told me earlier you want to lead with LaunchOps and add Studio as a capability. The site should express that hierarchy. Track 1 = the lead product. Track 2 = the expansion product. Putting LaunchOps as Track 1 aligns the site to the business strategy instead of contradicting it.
3. It matches buyer urgency. A prospect landing on your homepage has two possible mental states: "I have something broken and I need help fast" (LaunchOps urgency) or "I'm thinking about starting a project" (Studio patience). The urgent buyer needs to see their lane first, because urgency doesn't scroll past irrelevance to find what it needs. Patient buyers will find Studio whether it's listed first or second.
